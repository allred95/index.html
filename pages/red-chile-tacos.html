if your looking for a quick an easy meal that the whole family will enjoy? try this twist on the classic street taco!

for this dish you will need the following

    for the tacos

    1 pound boneless skinless chicken thighs

    olive oil

    salt

    pepper

    1 pouch of Frontera red chile enchilada sauce (or similar)

    one 14 ounce can pinto beans (rinsed and drained)

    for the creamy corn

    1/4 cup mayo

    2 tablespoons cream cheese

    2 cups frozen or fresh sweet corn

    1 clove garlic, grated

    1/2 teaspoon salt

    1 teaspoon cumin

    1 teaspoon chili powder

    lime juice to taste

    for the picled onion

    1 red onion, thinly sliced

    1/2 cup red wine vinegar

    1/2 cup water

    1 teaspoon salt

    1 tablespoon sugar

    for the extras

    corn tortillas (I like to quickly pan-fry mine in a shallow skillet of hot oil to make them extra soft)

    cilantro

INSTRUCTIONS

For the pickled onions:
    Transfer red onion slices to a jar.
    Fill the jar half to three-fourths of the way with red wine vinegar and the rest of the way with water.
    Add your salt and sugar.
    Shake a few times and set in the fridge while you make everything else.
For the chicken:
    Heat a skillet over medium-high heat.
    Add olive oil.
    Add the chicken and season with salt and pepper.
    Saut√© for 7-10 minutes, or until cooked through.
    Remove from the heat and shred or cut into smaller pieces.
    Add chicken pieces back to the pan with the sauce and the beans.
    Simmer gently until sauce has thickened and it looks ready to be taco filling.
For the corn:
    Mix all ingredients in a small bowl.
    Taste and adjust.
For the tacos:
    Fill tortillas with the chicken mixture.
    Top with the creamy corn and pickled red onions.
    Finish with cilantro. Mwah.
